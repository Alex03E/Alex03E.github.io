Lake Ontario Water-Level Regulation: Multi-Model Hydrologic Analysis
MATH 142
Sensitivity Analysis Lead · Author
Developed and compared three outflow regulation models for Lake Ontario — a baseline hydrologic reconstruction, a Plan-2014-style rule-based policy, and a Newton's-method optimization approach — using a monthly water-balance framework. Authored the full Sensitivity Analysis section and conducted systematic parameter experiments. Model 3 achieved the lowest stakeholder penalty score (2.033) and reduced threshold exceedances to 6.4%, versus 19.3% for the historical baseline.

Ames Housing Price Prediction: Regression Modeling & Variable Selection
STATS 101A
Statistical Modeling & Analysis
Built a high-dimensional regression model to predict house sale prices from 80+ predictors in the Ames Housing dataset. Applied Box-Cox transformation, engineered interaction features, and performed variable selection via forward/backward AIC and BIC. Final model achieved R² = 0.808 on 7,000 observations, with all retained predictors significant at α = 0.05. Iteratively resolved violations of linearity, normality, and heteroscedasticity.
